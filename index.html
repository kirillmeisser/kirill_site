About me

A little about myself

My name is Kirill Meisser, I am a computer vision engineer and cofounder of Sherlock. I have dual citizenship (Swiss and Russian) and was born in the cold yet beautiful city of St. Petersburg, Russia. Over the years I have moved three times: first to Mallorca, Spain, then to Lugano, Switzerland, and lastly to Zürich, where I currently reside.

These moves let me learn different languages and cultures and to swiftly adapt to new environments. Since I was a child I've had a great passion for technology and mathematics. I'm a driven, cheerful person who likes to have a good laugh, while always aiming to do my best at whatever I'm working on.

Portfolio

My projects

A selection of computer-vision and deep-learning systems I've built across manufacturing, medicine and privacy.

Automated Rivet Analysis

An algorithm that, given the cross-section of a rivet, computes 10 measurement overlays used in car-body construction to judge connection stability. Deployed at several car manufacturers with an estimated 60× workflow speed-up.

Clinching Connection Analysis

A related algorithm for the clinching process. From a cross-section image it outputs 11 measurements that help professionals decide whether a connection is stable and whether production meets quality controls.

Welding Analysis

A suite of models for analysing different weld types — the challenge being the sheer number of weld variations that need to be recognised and measured reliably.

Face Anonymization

A tool that reliably detects faces and occludes the eye region for minimally invasive anonymization. Trained on diverse poses, occlusions and ethnicities to combat bias and class imbalance.

Metal Structure Analysis

A set of models analysing properties of metal structures — phase fraction, grain size and porosity — to better understand the material and verify it meets the requirements for our application.
